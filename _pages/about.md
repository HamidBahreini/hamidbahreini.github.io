[cite_start]I am a **PhD Student in Computer Engineering** at **Polytechnique Montréal**[cite: 5], where I work under the supervision of **Prof. Giovanni Beltrame** and **Dr. [cite_start]Amir Hajzargarbashi**[cite: 12, 13]. [cite_start]My research is conducted in collaboration with the **National Research Council Canada (NRC)**, focusing on the frontier of **Intelligent Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR)** systems[cite: 6, 10].

Current Research & Expertise
[cite_start]My doctoral research addresses complex challenges in robotics by developing a new class of cognitive manufacturing equipment: **AMRs capable of complex welding in unstructured environments**[cite: 10]. I am particularly interested in:
* **Robotics & SLAM:** Developing robust navigation and mapping solutions[cite: 41, 42].
* [cite_start]**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI):** Enhancing operator safety through innovative motion planning and safety indices[cite: 37, 52].
* [cite_start]**Optimization:** Implementing Genetic Algorithms and AI-driven signal processing for robotic systems[cite: 74, 78].

Honors & Achievements
* **Opsidian Scholarship Recipient (2025-2028)** for doctoral studies[cite: 23].
* [cite_start]**3rd Place Winner** in the **European Space Agency (ESA) ESRIC Resources Challenge**, where I led the mechanical design of a lunar resource utilization robotic system[cite: 24, 25].
* [cite_start]**Fully Funded PhD Position** at NRC, awarded for rapid research progress and active contributions[cite: 11, 26].
* Ranked in the **Top 1.5%** of over 284,000 participants in the National Bachelor's Entrance Exam[cite: 29].

Before starting my PhD, I transitioned from a strong background in **Mechanical Engineering** (M.Sc. from Isfahan University of Technology) to Computer Engineering[cite: 14, 15, 27]. My professional journey includes years of experience as an **R&D Expert**, designing semi-automatic industrial machinery and cobotic systems[cite: 79, 82, 86, 89].

Beyond research, I am an active volunteer in the student community at Polytechnique Montréal and have a passion for organizing scientific events that bridge the gap between technology and society[cite: 54, 56, 61].
